What is PAW?

PAW is a student-led initiative using technology to transform the lives of stray dogs and cats. With safe, affordable, and interactive robotic companions, we help strays learn, socialize, and prepare for adoption, while supporting overwhelmed shelters.

Problem • Vision • Mission

Problem

It is estimated that there are more than 600 million stray dogs and cats worldwide, according to the World Organisation for Animal Health (WOAH). Shelters work hard to help, but they simply don’t have the space, staff, or resources to keep up. This leaves millions of strays living in harsh conditions, facing hunger, loneliness, lack of exercise, and unsafe environments. Even those that do make it into shelters often face overcrowding, limited care, and boredom. Over time, this can lead to poor health and stressed or aggressive behavior, making it even harder for them to be adopted into loving homes.

Vision

We see a world where every stray animal has the opportunity to be healthy, cared for, and adoptable through the help of innovative robotic companions that provide consistent training, comfort, and interaction.

Mission

  • Train stray dogs and cats to improve their behavior
  • Provide comfort to reduce loneliness and stress in shelters
  • Increase adoption rates by helping animals become friendlier and more adaptable to homes
  • Support local shelters by reducing the workload on overburdened staff and volunteers
